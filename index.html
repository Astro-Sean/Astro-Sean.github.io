I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Extraterrestrial Physics (MPE) in Garching. I work on high-energy astrophysical transients: multi-wavelength photometry, X-ray data, and spectra tied to massive stars and core-collapse explosions. I care about precursor activity, circumstellar interaction, and what wide-field surveys show about late stages of massive-star evolution.

I build Python pipelines for large, irregular time series (survey interfaces, forced photometry, uncertainties, reproducible workflows under real observing limits). I work with ZTF, the Rubin LSST transient community, and eROSITA. I started the PRESTO follow-up group and publish tools such as AUTOPHOT when others can reuse the analysis.

Positions, software, teaching, and outreach are listed in my CV (PDF).
