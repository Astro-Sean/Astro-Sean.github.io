Precursor Activity Preceding Interacting Supernovae I: Bridging the Gap with SN 2022mop

Brennan, S. J., Barmentloo, S., Schulze, S., et al. · Submitted to A&A · arXiv:2503.08768

arXiv abstract arXiv PDF DOI

Summary

Some interacting supernovae are preceded months or years earlier by a weaker outburst at the same place on the sky. This study focuses on SN 2022mop in the galaxy IC 1496: we trace an earlier phase consistent with a stripped-envelope transient, then a later brightening as a Type IIn supernova—again at the same coordinates—where the ejecta crash into surrounding gas. The analysis combines astrometry, light curves, and modelling to argue that both episodes belong to one stellar story.

Why it matters

If precursors and later SNe are genuinely linked, we can use pre-explosion variability as a forecast of upcoming interaction, not just a curiosity. That changes how we plan follow-up and how we count “failed” or repeated eruptions in a population. Bridging one precursor to one clear interacting SN also constrains how much mass stars toss off in the final phases of evolution—an open problem in massive-star theory.
