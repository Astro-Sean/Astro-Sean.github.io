Spectroscopic observations of progenitor activity 100 days before a Type Ibn supernova

Brennan, S. J., Sollerman, J., Irani, I., et al. · Astronomy & Astrophysics 684, L18 (2024)

arXiv abstract arXiv PDF Journal (DOI) NASA ADS

Summary

Type Ibn supernovae show narrow helium lines from the explosion crashing into material the star lost before death. This paper presents ZTF imaging plus spectra from NOT, Keck, and Gemini for SN 2023fyq. We caught the system roughly 100 days before explosion, when the star was already putting out extra light and changing helium lines. The data follow the event through peak and into late times, where behaviour consistent with a normal core-collapse supernova appears.

Why it matters

Astronomers still debate how massive stars shed mass and what that implies seconds before collapse. A well-sampled precursor at the same object as the SN links pre-explosion activity directly to the final blast—something wide-field surveys are only now making common. The work also shows how asymmetric, dense circumstellar material shapes what we see in Type Ibn events, which helps interpret future high-cadence data from facilities like ZTF and LSST.
