Peer-reviewed works where I was first author (I led analysis, writing, and coordination unless noted). Each entry below has three short paragraphs (background, science, and impact) and links to a dedicated page with the journal or arXiv version, the same overview, and (where available) an inline PDF plus arXiv HTML figure anchors.

  • Spectroscopic observations of progenitor activity 100 days before a Type Ibn supernova (SN 2023fyq)

    A&A 684, L18 (2024)

    Background. Massive stars shed gas before they explode; Type Ibn supernovae show that material through narrow helium lines when the blast hits it. Catching the star before collapse is still unusual, and linking that signal to the final supernova tests ideas about late-stage mass loss.

    Science. We use ZTF discovery plus NOT, Keck, and Gemini spectra of SN 2023fyq, including data taken about 100 days before explosion when the progenitor was already luminous and showing evolving He I lines. The sequence runs through peak and late times, where the behaviour matches a genuine core-collapse supernova and points to dense, asymmetric circumstellar material.

    Impact. The work ties real pre-explosion activity to the same terminal explosion that wide-field surveys now catch end to end. That feeds models of which stars make Type Ibn events and how to interpret high-cadence transient streams from ZTF, LSST, and follow-up networks.

  • Precursor Activity Preceding Interacting Supernovae I: Bridging the Gap with SN 2022mop

    Submitted to A&A | arXiv:2503.08768

    Background. Some interacting supernovae may be preceded by weaker eruptions at the same place on the sky. If those pairs are real, they bridge "precursor" variability and full supernova interaction, but clear examples are still needed.

    Science. We study SN 2022mop in IC 1496: an earlier phase consistent with a stripped-envelope transient, then a later brightening as a Type IIn supernova at the same astrometric position. Astrometry, light curves, and modelling connect the two episodes as one stellar sequence.

    Impact. Linked precursor plus SN events inform how often stars throw off shells before final collapse, how we should rank follow-up when precursors appear, and how population studies count terminal versus repeated eruptions.

  • SN 2021adxl: A luminous nearby interacting supernova in an extremely low-metallicity environment

    A&A 690, A259 (2024)

    Background. Most well-studied core-collapse supernovae live in galaxies with roughly solar or higher metallicity. Explosions in extremely metal-poor hosts are rarer in the local sample, yet they matter for early cosmic star formation and for testing winds and mass loss when stars contain few heavy elements.

    Science. SN 2021adxl is a bright, nearby supernova whose emission is strongly shaped by interaction with circumstellar gas. We present multi-band photometry, spectra, and host imaging to characterize the transient and its very low-metallicity environment.

    Impact. The case shows whether interaction strengths, progenitor clues, and host properties match patterns seen at higher Z, which matters for extrapolating supernova physics to young galaxies and for diversifying the observational training set used in theory and cosmology-related systematics.

  • The Automated Photometry of Transients pipeline (AUTOPHOT)

    A&A 667, A62 (2022)

    Background. Time-domain astronomy runs on light curves built from many telescopes and programmes. Forced photometry (measuring flux at a known position even when a source is faint) is central to survey follow-up, but results must be reproducible and easy to share.

    Science. AUTOPHOT is a Python pipeline that automates photometry for transients, including forced measurements, uncertainty treatment, and workflows aligned with survey-style data. The paper documents design choices, performance tests, and practical use.

    Impact. A published, citable pipeline lets other groups rerun the same analysis, compare methods fairly, and build on a common baseline. That is standard in many fields and increasingly expected in optical transient work. It also underpins several observational projects on this site.

  • The impostor revealed: SN 2016jbu was a terminal explosion

    A&A 664, L18 (2022)

    Background. Very luminous outbursts were sometimes called "supernova impostors": giant eruptions that mimic supernovae but were thought to leave the star intact. If true terminal explosions are mis-classified as impostors, rates and energy budgets for massive stars drift off course.

    Science. This Letter re-evaluates AT 2016jbu / SN 2016jbu using spectral evolution and late-time data. The evidence favours a genuine core-collapse supernova rather than a non-terminal impostor event.

    Impact. The case is a concrete reminder that long-lived transients need late-time confirmation before entering population studies. It sharpens criteria used in archival searches and theoretical modelling of luminous, interacting events.

  • Photometric and spectroscopic evolution of the interacting transient AT 2016jbu (Gaia16cfr)

    MNRAS 513, 5642 (2022)

    Background. AT 2016jbu is a long-duration, luminous transient powered by interaction between fast ejecta and slower circumstellar material. A full observational record is required before physical models can be tested in detail.

    Science. This paper (the first of two on the same object) delivers multi-colour light curves and a time-resolved spectral sequence from discovery through multiple peaks and decline phases.

    Impact. The data set is the empirical benchmark any theory of the explosion must reproduce; it also pairs directly with the companion paper on progenitor, environment, and modelling for a complete picture of one well-observed event.

  • Progenitor, environment, and modelling of the interacting transient AT 2016jbu (Gaia16cfr)

    MNRAS 513, 5666 (2022)

    Background. Light curves and spectra alone rarely fix a unique progenitor. Connecting observations to stellar models needs pre-explosion constraints, host context, and simple physical modelling, especially for complex interacting transients such as AT 2016jbu.

    Science. This companion paper combines archival imaging, host-galaxy information, and modelling to constrain the progenitor channel, mass-loss history, and structure of the circumstellar material around the event.

    Impact. Together with the photometry paper, it shows how modern transient studies often split into a phenomenology paper plus an interpretation paper, turning raw data into quantitative limits useful for comparing with other interacting SNe and with stellar-evolution theory.
