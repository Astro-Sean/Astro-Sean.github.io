Peer-reviewed works where I was first author (I led analysis, writing, and coordination unless noted). Each page links to the journal or arXiv version and summarizes the science. Paper pages include an inline PDF where the browser allows it (figures match the PDF), plus links to the arXiv HTML article for the same panels.

  • Spectroscopic observations of progenitor activity 100 days before a Type Ibn supernova (SN 2023fyq)

    A&A 684, L18 (2024)

    What it is: ZTF discovery and multi-telescope spectra of a Type Ibn SN, including observations about 100 days before explosion when the star was already bright and showing evolving helium lines. Why it matters: ties real pre-SN activity to the final blast—key for how massive stars lose mass and what wide-field surveys can catch before collapse.

  • Precursor Activity Preceding Interacting Supernovae I: Bridging the Gap with SN 2022mop

    Submitted to A&A · arXiv:2503.08768

    What it is: follow-up of a weaker outburst and then a Type IIn supernova at the same sky position in IC 1496, with astrometry and modelling linking the two episodes. Why it matters: shows how a “precursor” can foreshadow later interaction—useful for planning follow-up and for counting linked eruptions in stellar populations.

  • SN 2021adxl: A luminous nearby interacting supernova in an extremely low-metallicity environment

    A&A 690, A259 (2024)

    What it is: photometry, spectra, and host context for a bright, nearby interacting SN in an extremely low-metallicity galaxy. Why it matters: most SN samples are metal-rich; this event tests whether interaction and progenitor physics look the same when the host has almost no heavy elements—relevant for early galaxies and stellar evolution.

  • The Automated Photometry of Transients pipeline (AUTOPHOT)

    A&A 667, A62 (2022)

    What it is: the methods paper for AUTOPHOT—Python software for forced photometry, realistic uncertainties, and survey-style transient follow-up. Why it matters: published pipelines make light curves reproducible and comparable across teams, which the transient field is still standardizing.

  • The impostor revealed: SN 2016jbu was a terminal explosion

    A&A 664, L18 (2022)

    What it is: a short Letter arguing that AT 2016jbu was a true core-collapse supernova, not a non-destructive “impostor,” using spectra and late-time behaviour. Why it matters: mis-labels skew population studies; this case shows how late data can flip the classification of long-lived transients.

  • Photometric and spectroscopic evolution of the interacting transient AT 2016jbu (Gaia16cfr)

    MNRAS 513, 5642 (2022)

    What it is: multi-colour light curves and a full spectral sequence for the long-lived interacting transient AT 2016jbu (first of two papers). Why it matters: sets the observational baseline any model of the event must match; pairs with the progenitor/environment companion.

  • Progenitor, environment, and modelling of the interacting transient AT 2016jbu (Gaia16cfr)

    MNRAS 513, 5666 (2022)

    What it is: companion to the photometry paper—progenitor clues, host environment, and modelling for AT 2016jbu. Why it matters: turns light curves into quantitative limits on mass loss and circumstellar structure; shows the usual split between “data” and “interpretation” papers.
