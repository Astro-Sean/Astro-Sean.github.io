Progenitor, environment, and modelling of the interacting transient AT 2016jbu (Gaia16cfr)

Brennan, S. J., Fraser, M., Johansson, J., et al. · MNRAS 513, 5666 (2022)

arXiv abstract arXiv PDF Journal (DOI)

Summary

This is the companion paper to the photometry and spectroscopy study of AT 2016jbu. Here we combine pre-explosion imaging, host-galaxy data, and simple physical modelling to constrain the kind of star that exploded, how much material surrounded it, and how that circumstellar medium shapes the observed emission.

Why it matters

Light curves alone rarely fix a unique progenitor; adding environment and models turns a “pretty picture” into quantitative limits on mass loss and explosion energy. Together with the first paper, the pair shows how modern transient astronomy often needs two papers—one for phenomenology, one for interpretation—to tell a full story. That template is useful for MSc projects that combine data and modelling.
