The impostor revealed: SN 2016jbu was a terminal explosion

Brennan, S. J., Elias-Rosa, N., et al. · Astronomy & Astrophysics 664, L18 (2022)

arXiv abstract arXiv PDF Journal (DOI)

Overview

Background. Very luminous outbursts were sometimes called "supernova impostors": giant eruptions that mimic supernovae but were thought to leave the star intact. If true terminal explosions are mis-classified as impostors, rates and energy budgets for massive stars drift off course.

Science. This Letter re-evaluates AT 2016jbu / SN 2016jbu using spectral evolution and late-time data. The evidence favours a genuine core-collapse supernova rather than a non-terminal impostor event.

Impact. The case is a concrete reminder that long-lived transients need late-time confirmation before entering population studies. It sharpens criteria used in archival searches and theoretical modelling of luminous, interacting events.
