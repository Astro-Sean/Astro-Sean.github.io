SN 2021adxl: A luminous nearby interacting supernova in an extremely low-metallicity environment

Brennan, S. J., et al. · Astronomy & Astrophysics 690, A259 (2024)

Journal (DOI) A&A PDF ADS

Summary

SN 2021adxl is a luminous, nearby supernova whose light is powered partly by collision with dense gas around the progenitor—an interacting supernova. The host galaxy has very low metallicity—it formed from gas with few heavy elements compared with the Milky Way. We present multi-band photometry, spectra, and imaging of the host to describe how the transient evolves and how its environment differs from typical SN sites.

Why it matters

Most well-studied core-collapse SNe live in galaxies closer to solar metallicity. Events in extremely metal-poor hosts test whether mass loss, winds, and circumstellar shells behave the same way when stars contain fewer heavy elements—relevant for early galaxies and for linking supernova diversity to progenitor chemistry. A bright, nearby example is also easier for students and telescopes worldwide to study in detail.
